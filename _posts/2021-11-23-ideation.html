For the final project IxD, I'm going make small noise intoner boxes with a digital interface to make a little noise orchestra.

In my undergrad thesis, I made an enitrely digital version of the intonarumori - it was interesting, but I wanted something a little more tactile.

The Noises

Users will be able to play one sample in one of six categories of noises (as specified in The art of noises)

Technology Interface

The interface for playing the sound samples will be on MaxMSP. Users will rotate through different categories of noises and play one type of sound sample at a time. They'll be able to alter different parameters of noises.

The MaxMSP software will interface with an Arduino. Users be able to control the sound samples through physical components connected to the Arduino.

I might want to use a small dev board, but we'll see!

The Box

I want the box to be relatively small - like a cigar box.

It will have several sliders/dials, one to control pitch, another for volume, and one more for speed.

For now, the prototype will be made of cardboard. Conceptually, I'd like the box to have a clear top so users can see the components.

Next steps

The next steps are trying this out on a breadboard with the MaxMSP interface, and then gather all the sound samples I'd like to use.
